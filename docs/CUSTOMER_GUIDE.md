# Fraud Immunity Lab — Customer Guide

## What is the Immunity Score?

Your **Immunity Score** (0–100) measures how well your fraud detection infrastructure would hold up against the most sophisticated attacks targeting financial institutions like yours.

A score of **80+** means your systems would detect most known attack types with high confidence. A score below **60** signals meaningful gaps that a real attacker could exploit.

Unlike reactive fraud metrics (transaction loss rate, chargeback rate), the Immunity Score is *proactive* — it tells you what you're protected against *before* an attack happens.

---

## How the Score is Calculated

Your Immunity Score is a weighted composite of four components:

### Detection Coverage (40% of score)
> *"What fraction of known attack types would your systems actually catch?"*

The lab runs synthetic versions of real fraud attacks against your detection stack. For each attack type (phishing, money laundering, account takeover, etc.), it measures how reliably your systems flag them.

- **Green (≥ 90%)**: Your systems reliably detect this attack type
- **Orange (70–89%)**: Partial detection — some variants slip through
- **Red (< 70%)**: Significant gap — this attack type is poorly covered

### False Positive Health (30% of score)
> *"How often do your systems flag legitimate customers as fraudulent?"*

Every false alarm costs you: customer friction, manual review time, and lost revenue from blocked legitimate transactions. This component measures the false positive rate on real transaction patterns.

- **Green**: < 2% false positive rate (acceptable for production)
- **Orange**: 2–5% — customers are experiencing friction
- **Red**: > 5% — significant operational burden

### Model Freshness (20% of score)
> *"Are your behavioral profiles keeping up with how customers actually transact?"*

Customer spending patterns evolve. Seasonal changes, new payment channels, and life events (relocations, new jobs) shift what "normal" looks like. Stale profiles — older than 30 days — reduce detection accuracy.

- **Green**: ≥ 90% of profiles refreshed within 30 days
- **Orange**: 70–89% fresh
- **Red**: < 70% — many profiles are stale and unreliable

### Scenario Diversity (10% of score)
> *"How broad is your red-team coverage?"*

A high Detection Coverage score for three attack types doesn't help you against the fourth. This component rewards testing breadth — ensuring no known attack category is left untested.

## Reading Your Dashboard

### Score Hero
The large number at the top is your current Immunity Score. The sparkline shows how your score has trended over the last 30 days. Look for:
- **Upward trend** after red-team runs (expected — detection improves as gaps are identified and closed)
- **Sudden drops** (usually mean freshness degraded — trigger a profile refresh)

### Component Breakdown
Four cards showing each sub-score with a progress bar. Click the weight tag (e.g. "40% weight") for an explanation of how that component feeds into the total.

### Attack Scenario Map
A table of every attack type the lab has tested. For each:
- **Recall** = fraction of synthetic attacks detected (higher is better)
- **Scenarios run** = number of unique attack variants tested
- **Status** = whether Hard Rule #6 is satisfied (≥ 90% recall required for production)

Red-highlighted rows are active gaps — attacks your systems would likely miss.

### Fraud Ring Visualizer
A graph of account relationship clusters flagged as suspicious by the Neo4j community detection algorithm. High-risk nodes (displayed in pulsing red) share IP addresses or devices with known fraud indicators. Click any node to see the signal details.

### Alert Feed
Real-time stream of transactions and login events that triggered the drift detector. Each alert shows:
- **Risk score**: cosine distance from the account's clean behavioral baseline
- **Account token**: a pseudonymized identifier (never the raw account number)
- **Synthetic** tag: present if this alert was generated by a red-team run

### Red-Team Status
Shows the current state of the four background DAGs that keep your Immunity Score fresh:
- `attack_orchestrator`: generates and tests new attack scenarios
- `scenario_coverage_dag`: updates your coverage report
- `community_detection_dag`: refreshes fraud ring detection
- `profile_refresh_dag`: updates stale behavioral profiles

## What Triggers an Alert?

An alert fires when one of two conditions is met:

**1. Personal drift**: A transaction's behavioral embedding is significantly different from the account's historical pattern. Specifically, the cosine similarity between the new transaction and the account's baseline profile falls below 0.85 (or 0.90 for high-value accounts).

*Example*: An account that typically makes small, local grocery purchases suddenly initiates a $4,800 international wire at 3am from a new device.

**2. Suspicious pattern match**: The transaction embedding is highly similar (≥ 80% cosine similarity) to a known synthetic fraud pattern in the suspicious-profiles index.

*Example*: A sequence of small transactions at different merchants over 24 hours matches the "smurfing" pattern the red-team generated last week.

Both conditions can trigger simultaneously (a "both" drift type), which carries the highest confidence signal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the Immunity Score a guarantee?**
No. It is a leading indicator, not a guarantee. A score of 90 means your systems detected 90% of *known synthetic attack types* in the lab — it does not mean you are immune to all fraud. Novel attacks not yet in the scenario library will not be reflected until a red-team run covers them.

**Q: What does "synthetic" mean on an alert?**
Alerts tagged as synthetic were generated by the lab's own red-team attacks — not by real fraud. They appear in your feed so you can see how your detection system responds. Real production alerts have no synthetic tag.

**Q: Why did my score drop?**
The most common causes are:
1. Profile staleness (Model Freshness component dropped)
2. A new attack type was added to the library that you haven't tested yet (Scenario Diversity dropped)
3. A red-team run found a detection gap (Detection Coverage dropped)

Check the Component Breakdown for the sub-score that changed most.

**Q: How often does the score update?**
The score recomputes on demand (maximum cache staleness: 5 minutes) and is triggered automatically when a red-team DAG run completes.

**Q: Can I trigger a red-team run myself?**
Not directly from the dashboard in the beta. Contact your Customer Success Manager to trigger an on-demand run, or request access to the Airflow UI.

**Q: What data does the lab see?**
The lab sees only tokenized transaction data — never raw account numbers, names, or personal identifiers. All account IDs are pseudonymized before any embedding or graph operation. See your data processing agreement for the full data inventory.

## Interpreting Your Weekly Report

Every Monday morning you receive an automated weekly report. Key sections:

**Score trend**: Week-over-week average (not just the single point-in-time score). A stable or rising trend is healthy; a downward trend for two consecutive weeks should be investigated.

**Lowest recall attack types**: The three attack types where your detection recall is weakest. These are your highest-priority gaps.

**Recommended actions**: Prioritized list generated automatically based on your current score composition. Follow these in order — they are ranked by impact.

**LLM spend**: How much of your monthly AI budget has been used. Alert fires at 80% — if you're approaching that, consider whether your red-team schedule needs adjustment.
